How to Deal with Spoiled Meat

Meat that has spoiled poses a major health danger. Here are some vital pointers for handling it securely.

How to Identify Spoiled Meat

Slimy texture, bad smell, and dull color are common characteristics of spoiled meat. Make sure the meat is fresh by inspecting it before cooking.

Secure Elimination

If you suspect that your meat has gone bad, it's crucial to dispose of it safely. To help prevent bacteria and odors from spreading, place the spoilt meat in a sealed bag.

Cleaning Up

Thoroughly clean any surfaces that came into contact with the devastated meat after disposing of it. Cutting boards, knives, and worktops can be cleaned with hot, soapy water or a bleach solution.
